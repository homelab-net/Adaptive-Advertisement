ADAPTIVE RETAIL TARGET END-STATE VISION v1.0

Status
- Directional future-state vision for design and architecture alignment.
- Does not expand MVP scope or authorize implementation outside the active baseline.
- MVP execution, pilot gating, and release claims remain governed by the highest-revision roadmap, ICDs, TRD, V&V plan, and pilot protocol.

1. Purpose
This document defines the intended post-MVP end state for the Adaptive Retail Advertising System so that the MVP foundation is built on the correct long-term architecture. It describes the “house” the system is intended to become, while preserving the locked MVP constraints already established.

2. Product Vision
The intended final product is a privacy-first, local-first adaptive signage platform that uses live local audience-state signals and aggregate performance metrics to select among approved creative variants, compare outcomes through controlled A/B testing, and progressively favor the best-performing approved combinations of creative components for current audience conditions.

The system is not intended to become a freeform generative ad engine, a cloud-required adtech platform, or an identity-based targeting system. Its long-term value comes from bounded, explainable, privacy-preserving optimization of approved creative combinations while maintaining appliance-grade playback reliability and operator control.

3. End-State Product Statement
The final product should be understood as:

A privacy-first adaptive signage appliance and fleet platform that continuously improves approved on-screen advertising through local audience-aware delivery, controlled experimentation, aggregate business metrics, and human-governed optimization, without storing raw imagery, exposing raw frames, or making WAN connectivity a runtime dependency.

4. Permanent Non-Negotiable Constraints
These rules remain true in MVP and in the final product:
- All computer-vision inference remains local to the device.
- No raw image persistence.
- No raw frame or video egress.
- No identity recognition, biometric storage, or cross-visit tracking.
- Playback is a hard dependency; CV, analytics, DB, dashboard, and WAN are soft dependencies.
- The screen must never go blank.
- Manual override and safe mode must always exist.
- Adaptive behavior must remain inside approved policy space.
- The player may render only approved manifests.
- WAN is not required for runtime operation.
- Remote administration remains VPN-only unless later formally revised.
- Recovery follows restart-ladder logic; blind nightly reboot posture is prohibited.

5. End-State Functional Intent
5.1 Audience-Aware Delivery
The system should use local audience-state inputs to influence which approved creative assembly is displayed at a given moment. This includes:
- Real-time local adaptation using metadata-only audience-state signals.
- Stable confidence-gated switching.
- Freeze-on-uncertainty and graceful degradation.
- Preservation of stable playback behavior when audience confidence is weak, stale, or unavailable.

5.2 Structured Creative Assembly
The final product should deliver ads through structured, policy-constrained creative assembly rather than freeform generation. This includes:
- Approved templates.
- Approved image and design assets.
- Approved text elements.
- Approved variant groups.
- Approved style and emphasis modifiers within defined policy bounds.
- Deterministic render manifests suitable for offline-safe playback.

5.3 Controlled A/B Testing and Experimentation
The final product should include disciplined experimentation capabilities, not unconstrained multivariate chaos. This includes:
- Static-vs-adaptive comparison using the same approved creative library.
- Variant family comparison inside the same approved policy space.
- Controlled test windows and documented experiment conditions.
- Aggregate local metrics for comparing conditions.
- Promotion or reweighting decisions based on measured results.

The objective is to measure whether adaptive behavior creates a positive directional business signal without increasing support burden or eroding operator trust.

5.4 Measured Self-Optimization
The final product should move from rules-first decisioning toward bounded optimizer assistance. The optimization target is not “generate the best ad from scratch.” It is:

Select and weight the best-performing approved combination of creative components for a given audience-state context, using local aggregate performance signals and bounded experimentation.

Examples of future optimization surfaces:
- Template choice.
- Headline variant.
- Body or offer variant.
- CTA variant.
- Brand-approved style or color modifier.
- Hold time and switch timing.
- Rotation weight.
- Context-conditioned campaign eligibility.

5.5 Human-Governed Control Plane
The final product remains operator-controlled. It should provide:
- Upload, review, and approval workflows for assets and variants.
- Manual approval as the default gate for runtime eligibility.
- Ability to enable, pause, resume, or restrict adaptive behavior.
- Safe mode and fallback playlist control.
- Clear owner-facing analytics and system status.
- No approval bypass path.

5.6 Business-Usable Analytics
The analytics surface should remain clear, aggregate, and commercially useful rather than ML-lab oriented. It should support:
- Impressions and exposure opportunity counts.
- Dwell and attention-direction proxies.
- Static-vs-adaptive comparison results.
- Variant-family performance trends.
- Campaign and site-level summaries.
- Reliability, uptime, and support-burden reporting.
- Explainable reason codes and decision trace summaries suitable for operator review.

5.7 Reliability and Fallback Behavior
The system is intended to behave as an appliance, not a demo. End-state reliability expectations include:
- Always-on playback.
- Offline-safe local asset cache and fallback bundle.
- No visible blank-screen behavior during normal operation.
- Controlled switching with commercially acceptable visual smoothness.
- Player-first recovery before device reboot.
- Freeze or fallback behavior when CV, decisioning, creative, DB, dashboard, or network layers degrade.
- Supportable remote diagnosis and recovery without making cloud runtime a requirement.

5.8 Local-First Appliance Operation
The final product should remain usable as a stand-alone in-store appliance. This means:
- Local dashboard.
- Local storage.
- Local decisioning.
- Local analytics persistence.
- Local playback continuity even during WAN outages.
- Optional VPN-based remote administration and maintenance.

5.9 Fleet and Multi-Site Growth
The final product may later grow into a fleet layer above local runtime. This includes:
- Multi-location campaign coordination.
- Cross-store performance comparison.
- Fleet health and support visibility.
- Site, tenant, and device-level analytics rollups.
- Promotion of approved winning variant families across sites.
- Staged update and rollout rings.

This layer must remain additive. Cross-site features must not become a runtime dependency for the in-store appliance.

6. End-State Optimization Posture
The system’s future optimization posture should mature in the following order:

Phase A: Rules-first adaptive delivery
- Deterministic keep/switch/fallback logic.
- Explainable decisions.
- Frozen fallback when uncertain.

Phase B: Controlled pilot experimentation
- Static-vs-adaptive measurement.
- Same approved library in both conditions.
- Minimal, decision-useful metrics.

Phase C: Bounded optimizer assistance
- Conservative weighting or bandit-assisted ranking of approved variants.
- Hard caps, cooldowns, and confidence gates.
- Human review and rollback retained.

Phase D: Continuous bounded improvement
- Ongoing local reweighting among approved variants.
- Promotion of stronger performers within policy-approved bounds.
- Persistent explainability, auditability, and operator control.

7. Target Feature Set Summary
Top-level future feature areas:
- Audience-aware delivery.
- Structured creative assembly.
- Controlled A/B testing.
- Measured self-optimization.
- Human approval and override controls.
- Aggregate business analytics.
- Reliability-first playback and fallback behavior.
- Privacy-preserving local CV.
- WAN-independent local appliance runtime.
- Multi-site fleet analytics and campaign coordination.

8. Features Explicitly In Scope for the Final Product Direction
The following are appropriate future-state features if implemented within the permanent constraints:
- Local audience-aware switching based on metadata-only state.
- Approved adaptive text variation within bounded rules.
- Approved style/color emphasis variation within bounded rules.
- Controlled A/B testing of approved variants.
- Static-vs-adaptive comparison reporting.
- Variant reweighting based on aggregate outcomes.
- Cross-store comparison of adaptive lift, trend direction, uptime, and support burden.
- Fleet health views and remote support tooling over VPN.
- Central approval workspace for multi-site operators.
- Staged update rollout and rollback by ring.
- Benchmark-style reporting across sites, campaign families, or business cohorts using aggregate-only analytics.

9. Explicit Non-Goals Even in the Final Product
The final product is not intended to become:
- A freeform prompt-based ad generator.
- A synthetic image generation runtime.
- An unconstrained multivariate experimentation lab.
- A cloud-dependent runtime control plane.
- An identity-based targeting or surveillance platform.
- A repeat-visitor memory system.
- A product that exposes raw camera feeds to operators, clients, or remote admins.
- A product that allows automatic approval bypass or direct unreviewed creative publication.

10. Architectural Implications for MVP Foundation
Because this is the intended end state, the MVP foundation should already assume:
- Stable service boundaries.
- Versioned interface contracts.
- Canonical business objects for campaigns, templates, variant groups, assets, modifiers, approvals, manifests, sites, and devices.
- Append-only event logging for decision, playback, health, approval, and experiment events.
- Aggregate analytics persistence separate from playback-critical paths.
- Explainable decision traces.
- Approval workflow as a permanent control surface.
- Optimizer integration as a bounded layer on top of rules-first logic rather than a future replacement architecture.
- Fleet-ready identifiers such as tenant_id, site_id, device_id, campaign_id, creative_id, assembly_id, and schema_version.

11. Target Metrics Categories for the Final Product
The end-state system should measure only what is useful, explainable, and supportable.

11.1 Commercial / optimization metrics
- Dwell or attention-direction proxies.
- Variant performance deltas.
- Adaptive-vs-static directional performance change.
- Campaign-level trend summaries.
- Variant-family win/loss trends.
- Owner acceptance and trust indicators.

11.2 Reliability metrics
- Playback availability.
- Visible blank or error-screen time.
- Controlled switch quality.
- Time to recover playback.
- Fallback invocation rate.
- Service fault and restart patterns.

11.3 Operational metrics
- Remote triage time.
- On-site visit avoidance rate.
- Support burden by site.
- Update success and rollback outcomes.
- Camera and mount repeatability observations.

11.4 Privacy and governance metrics
- Zero retained imagery.
- Zero raw frame egress.
- Zero approval bypass events.
- Audit completeness for approvals, overrides, and recovery actions.

12. Maintenance and Support Notes for the Intended Product
The final product should remain supportable for a solo founder or very small team. That requires preserving the following maintenance posture:

12.1 Reproducible appliance build
- Maintain a reproducible base image and deployment stack.
- Treat the appliance as a managed product image, not an ad hoc install per customer.
- Keep startup order, health checks, and recovery behavior deterministic.

12.2 Golden image with first-boot provisioning
- A cloned/golden image approach is acceptable and desirable for scaling deployment.
- Unique per-device identity must be created or assigned at first boot.
- Do not clone final SSH host keys, WireGuard credentials, or unique device identity across customers.
- Default network posture should rely on customer-LAN DHCP rather than baked-in local static IP assumptions.

12.3 VPN-only remote support
- Founder support should use WireGuard-based remote access rather than public WAN exposure.
- SSH-over-VPN and tunneled admin workflows are acceptable internal support methods.
- Remote support tooling must not weaken the privacy boundary or create runtime WAN dependence.

12.4 Update and rollback discipline
- Updates should be signed, controlled, and rollback-capable.
- Rollout rings are preferable to broad uncontrolled fleet pushes.
- Update failure must not strand the device in an unrecoverable or blank-screen state.

12.5 Supportability-first design
- Operator workflows must be understandable without shell access.
- Dashboard and install flows should reduce founder intervention.
- Remote triage should be practical for common faults.
- Local runtime should continue even when remote admin is unavailable.

12.6 Evidence packaging
- The product should eventually produce customer-safe proof packs and operator-safe summaries rather than requiring raw engineering logs to explain value or reliability.

13. Design Guidance for Contributors
Designers and developers should use this document to understand the intended future shape of the product while preserving the locked MVP foundation. The key rule is:

Build the smallest correct foundation now, but build it so the bounded future system can grow on top of it without rewrite.

That means:
- Avoid convenience choices that create future rebuild traps.
- Prefer deterministic, typed, and documented interfaces.
- Favor explainable decisioning over opaque automation.
- Protect privacy and playback before adding sophistication.
- Separate business controls, optimization, creative policy, and playback responsibilities cleanly.

14. Final Framing
The end-state system is best framed as:

A privacy-first adaptive signage platform that continuously tests and improves approved ad variants locally, proves value against a static baseline, preserves operator control and appliance-grade playback, and later scales to explainable multi-site analytics and bounded optimization.
