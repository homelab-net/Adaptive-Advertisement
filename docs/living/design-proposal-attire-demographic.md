# Design Proposal — Attire Demographic Dimension (CRM-005)

**Status:** Approved 2026-04-02 (not yet implemented)

---

## Summary

Attire is a 10-bin gender-neutral clothing category classification added as a coarse demographic dimension alongside age_group and gender. CV models classify the dominant clothing style of observed individuals into one of ten categories covering the major retail contexts: formal, business_casual, casual, athletic, outdoor_technical, workwear_uniform, streetwear, luxury_premium, lounge_comfort, and smart_occasion. Each bin is represented as an aggregate probabilistic score over the observation window, following the identical privacy posture as gender and age_group. The 10 bins map to 10 user-selectable manifest audience tags. Cross-dimension combination rules (e.g., attire + age, attire + gender) are auto-generated by the rule_generator at a +3 priority bonus over single-dimension rules, enabling high-precision audience targeting for retail signage without introducing biometric data.

## Privacy Analysis

**Same posture as gender (CRM-003).** Attire classification is based on visible clothing appearance — texture, cut, color palette, and garment category — without reference to any biometric data. No face embeddings are computed. No body measurements are taken or stored. The classification is aggregate-only: a single probabilistic score per bin is computed across the observation window and is not disaggregated to individual persons.

**Suppressed with `demographics_suppressed`.** Attire bins are members of the demographics block and are subject to the same `demographics_suppressed` gate as age_group and gender. When `demographics_suppressed=True`, attire fields are omitted from the ICD-3 signal and are not evaluated by the policy engine.

**`additionalProperties: false` blocks unknown bins.** The attire object in both ICD-2 and ICD-3 schemas uses `additionalProperties: false`. This prevents unknown classification outputs (e.g., from future model versions with different bin sets) from silently flowing through the pipeline without a schema review.

**No face or body biometrics.** Clothing classification operates on the full-body region of the bounding box, not on the face crop. No biometric templates, skeletal measurements, or morphological features are stored. The `contains_face_embeddings: false` contract flag continues to apply.

**Coarse and probabilistic.** Each bin score is a float in [0.0, 1.0] representing the aggregate probability that observed individuals belong to that clothing category. Scores across bins need not sum to 1.0 due to multi-person windows and partial visibility. Consistent with PRIV-001..PRIV-005.

## Cross-Dimension Combination Rules

### `_DIMENSION_GROUPS` constant

The rule_generator defines a `_DIMENSION_GROUPS` constant that maps each audience tag to its demographic dimension:

```python
_DIMENSION_GROUPS = {
    # age dimension
    "young_adult_focus": "age",
    "middle_adult_focus": "age",
    "senior_focus": "age",
    # gender dimension
    "male_focus": "gender",
    "female_focus": "gender",
    # attire dimension
    "attire_formal": "attire",
    "attire_business_casual": "attire",
    "attire_casual": "attire",
    "attire_athletic": "attire",
    "attire_outdoor_technical": "attire",
    "attire_workwear_uniform": "attire",
    "attire_streetwear": "attire",
    "attire_luxury_premium": "attire",
    "attire_lounge_comfort": "attire",
    "attire_smart_occasion": "attire",
}
```

Tags not present in `_DIMENSION_GROUPS` (e.g., `attract`, `general_audience`) are not eligible for cross-dimension combination rule generation.

### `_CROSS_DIM_PRIORITY_BONUS = 3`

When a manifest specifies audience tags from two or more different dimensions, the rule_generator produces combination rules at priority **13** (base demographic priority 10 + cross-dimension bonus 3). If a time-of-day modifier is also present, the combined priority is **33** (base 30 + cross-dimension bonus 3).

Single-dimension rules continue to be generated at priority 10 (or 30 with time). The cross-dimension bonus reflects the higher specificity of a compound condition: a rule that requires both `attire_athletic` and `young_adult_focus` to be satisfied simultaneously is a stronger match than either condition alone.

### Example: manifest with `attire_athletic` and `young_adult_focus`

Given a manifest with audience tags `["attire_athletic", "young_adult_focus"]`, the rule_generator produces:

1. A single-dimension rule for `attire_athletic` at priority 10 with condition `attire_athletic_gte: 0.45` (plus auto-injected `attention_engaged_gte: 0.35`).
2. A single-dimension rule for `young_adult_focus` at priority 10 with its age condition (plus auto-injected attention gate).
3. A cross-dimension combination rule at priority **13** combining both conditions: `attire_athletic_gte: 0.45 AND age_young_adult_gte: <threshold> AND attention_engaged_gte: 0.35`.

The combination rule fires preferentially when both dimensions are satisfied, ensuring the most targeted ad is served when the audience precisely matches the campaign's intended profile.

## Threshold Rationale

Thresholds are assigned per CV reliability tier. Lower thresholds indicate higher model confidence at retail-signage distances and typical store lighting conditions.

**High tier — threshold 0.45:** Categories with visually distinctive and consistent clothing signatures: formal (suits, blazers), business_casual (collared shirts, slacks), casual (jeans, t-shirts, hoodies), athletic (branded activewear, performance fabrics). These categories have well-separated feature distributions at standard retail camera distances (1–5 meters). A 0.45 threshold requires moderate evidence — roughly equivalent to the majority of observed individuals appearing to match — before the condition fires.

**Moderate tier — threshold 0.50:** Categories that are visually distinctive but have higher inter-class overlap or are less frequently seen in general retail traffic: outdoor_technical (technical layers, harnesses, hiking boots), workwear_uniform (high-vis vests, scrubs, coveralls), streetwear (logos, oversized silhouettes, specific footwear). A 0.50 threshold requires clear majority evidence. These categories are reliably distinguishable but may be confused with adjacent categories (e.g., outdoor_technical vs. casual, streetwear vs. casual).

**Experimental tier — threshold 0.55:** Categories with either low base-rate in retail traffic, fine-grained visual distinctions that are difficult to classify from CCTV-distance imagery, or high dependency on fabric texture and accessory detail: luxury_premium (subtle tailoring cues, accessory quality), lounge_comfort (ambiguous at distance from casual), smart_occasion (cocktail vs. business_casual ambiguity). A 0.55 threshold requires strong evidence to reduce false-positive targeting. Operators using experimental-tier tags should validate performance in their specific deployment environment before relying on them for campaign decisions.

## `casual` Definition

The `casual` bin is defined strictly as everyday casual wear: jeans, t-shirts, hoodies, sneakers, and similarly non-specialized clothing with no strong category signal. It is **not** a catch-all or residual category.

The distinction is critical for model reliability. If `casual` is defined as "anything that doesn't fit another category," the model learns to dump ambiguous or low-confidence frames into the casual bin, inflating casual scores and degrading confidence across all other bins. Under the strict definition:

- An individual in ambiguous attire (e.g., visible from the waist up only, partial occlusion, unusual lighting) should produce **all-low bin scores** rather than a high casual score.
- The model should abstain (low across all bins) rather than guess.
- Rule conditions threshold at 0.45 for High-tier bins, which already requires positive evidence rather than absence-of-other-categories.

Operators should treat a low-scoring casual result as "insufficient evidence for attire targeting" rather than as a positive match for casual wear. The dashboard and analytics pipeline should surface this distinction clearly in documentation and operator guidance.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] ICD-2 schema (`cv-observation.schema.json`) includes optional `attire` block inside `demographics` with 10 float fields (one per bin), `additionalProperties: false`.
- [ ] ICD-3 schema (`audience-state-signal.schema.json`) includes optional `state.demographics.attire` object with the same 10 float fields, `additionalProperties: false`.
- [ ] `input-cv` service defines `ObservationAttire` model with 10 bin fields and builder extraction logic; emits attire block only when demographics are not suppressed and the attire model is available.
- [ ] `audience-state` service aggregates attire bins over the observation window using the same smoothing approach as age_group and gender; includes attire in the ICD-3 signal under `state.demographics`.
- [ ] Policy engine includes all 10 attire condition fields in `_DEMOGRAPHIC_CONDITION_FIELDS`; attire conditions are subject to `demographics_suppressed` gate identically to age and gender conditions.
- [ ] `rule_generator` defines `_DIMENSION_GROUPS` mapping all 10 attire tags to the `"attire"` dimension; defines `_CROSS_DIM_PRIORITY_BONUS = 3`; generates cross-dimension combination rules at priority 13 (or 33 with time) when a manifest contains tags from two or more distinct dimension groups.
- [ ] `rule_generator` defines per-tier thresholds (High=0.45, Moderate=0.50, Experimental=0.55) applied to the appropriate bins when generating audience conditions.
- [ ] `AudienceSnapshot` ORM model has 10 nullable float columns (one per attire bin); Alembic migration 0004 creates them (combined with attention columns from CRM-004).
- [ ] All 10 attire audience tags are present in `AUDIENCE_TAGS` and generate valid rule conditions with correct thresholds in the rule_generator test suite.
- [ ] Cross-dimension combination rule generation is verified by at least 8 test cases covering all dimension-pair combinations (age+attire, gender+attire, age+gender+attire).
- [ ] Integration privacy audit test confirms attire fields are absent from all published signals when `demographics_suppressed=True`.
- [ ] `casual` bin validation: test confirms that an input with all-zero attire probabilities does not produce a rule match for any attire audience tag (i.e., no catch-all promotion of the casual bin).
